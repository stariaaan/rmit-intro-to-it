.project idea: donation management

#overview

I want to create an application based on the concept of "Share all that we have," which is critical during this lockdown. Residents in one area can donate food, clothing, and medicine to the local checkpoints. At the same time, people in need of resources can check to see if there is anything they require at their local checkpoints, call the number listed in the description, or chat on Zalo/Viber if they are unable to make a phone call. People can use this application to ask for help from their neighbors when their needs are unique and require immediate assistance.

#motivation

My motivation for this application is because of a man who attempted to go out after the curfew to obtain an oxygen cylinder for his son, who has hemangioma liver, at home because he only knew of one reliable source (Ngoc & Vu, 2021). I have also tested positive for COVID-19 and have been treating it at home, so I understand how difficult it is to obtain medicine if the government is not present to assist. That is why I am hoping to create this application to support others who are in need of assistance during this difficult time.

The flow of this application (Figure 1. Homescreen) is described in Figure 2. Application flow.


There are two different flows for two types of user: donators and receivers.
- Donators can apply for donation using the page in Figure 3. Donation page.
- Receivers can apply for requests in Figure 4. Request page.
Once a contributor has applied for donations, the district/ward/local government in the area will decide who will collect the donations and deliver them to the nearest checkpoints/checkpoints that need donations.


Anyone can request something they need using the screen in Figure 4. Request page. If what they need is available after they apply for it, the local government will transport it from the checkpoints to the requested address.

#cloud computing

In my project ideas, I mentioned that I would use Firebase for the development. Firebase is a cloud service that provide real-time and collaborative applications. Using cloud computing services to host a website or an application has become a common practice in recent years.

Cloud computing delivers computing services (including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and more) remotely over the Internet to provide more resources and flexibility to its users. Cloud computing lets users choose as many services as they need and charge only what they use, while eliminate the worries of investing in hardware and maintenance. This feature helps lower operating costs, allowing the company to run more efficiently and scale the operation as needed due to its scalability, making it less expensive than the traditional-based infrastructure for small organizations. While there are many cloud computing providers, the services often fall into three major categories:

IaaS offers servers and storage in the Cloud. Customers of IaaS are tech companies with thorough IT expert knowledge because the purpose of IaaS is to have access to computing power without having to worry about hardware installation or maintenance.
PaaS supports the development of software for computer systems that require a platform by including software development technologies. Companies can use this service from writing software to hosting the finished products and make them available to internet users.
SaaS is a software delivery model. It is the largest and most developed area of Cloud-based services. Rather than installing on a computer's hard drive, it is a program or a package of applications available in the Cloud.

Many companies are using cloud computing, making one foreseeable change in the near future is that the Cloud providers will offer more data storage with lower prices. For individuals, it increases the computing resources for the users without worrying about hardware.

Cloud computing makes daily use services more accessible:

- Store, back up and recover data. Just about ten years ago, USB and HDD were the standards for storing and transferring data. Nowadays, almost everyone has a cloud storage account to save and share at least 15 gigabytes of data.

- Streaming services/social media platforms. Most of the social media and entertainment sites are cloud-based applications. People can now stream what is happening for others who live in the other half of the world. Moreover, streaming services, such as Netflix and Spotify, offer people ways to entertain and connect with others.

- Education. Take Coursera, for example, a cloud-based education service that offers university courses for its users, who can study anytime and anywhere.

Cloud computing has changed the way individuals and companies manage data, as well as communication. Working from home is the norm for today society, many services have to adapt to these circumstances. Cloud-based services, such as Microsoft Onedrive and Google Drive, make collaboration more straightforward than ever. Employees from the same organization or students in the same class could view and share information quickly, increasing productivity and giving people more flexibility. Especially during COVID-19, its ability to let people work and study, as well as spreading information, is improving every day.

As the need for these services increase, more inventions would be created. Some of the services and hardware to use them only available to people from the middle class and higher. However, during this pandemic where elementary students, even kindergarten students, must study online, it is challenging for every family to prepare all the needed technologies.

More inventions involving Cloud computing will evolve over the next few years, accessible for people from all circumstances. I believe that the more accessible computing is, the more technology-based life will become. From the experience I have studying at RMIT, Cloud-based technologies have been giving me an adaptable study environment. The learning management system combined with communication tools, Instructure and Teams, enable students to participate in class, rewatch the lecture videos to study at their own pace, sharing working files with other students via Cloud-based storage.

Besides online study, Cloud-based shopping sites and delivery applications provide people with easy access to buying essential goods. Without the need to protect the physical server during lockdowns, these companies could let their employees to work from home and finding ways to adapt to the new normal, letting users like my family and myself continue to use their products.

#cybersecurity & privacy

Security and privacy are always the concern of internet users, from internet browsing activity to using cloud-based services. My project needs a lot of effort in taking care of the data, since they are personal and sensitive information.

Cyber security also referred to as information technology security or electronic data security. It is the practice of defending computers, servers, mobile devices, and data from malicious attacks. The term is used in a variety of contexts, ranging from business to mobile computing, and can be classified into a few general categories.

Network security is defending computer networks against attackers. Application security is keeping software and devices safe from threats because a compromised application may expose its data. Data integrity and privacy are protected by information security. The processes and decisions for handling and protecting data assets are operational security. Operational security includes the permissions that users have when connecting to a network as well as the procedures that govern how and where data can be stored or shared. By failing to follow good security practices, anyone can inadvertently introduce a virus into a secured system.

In the near future, AI could be a very big part in improving security. Nowadays, we can see smartphones and personal laptops using biometric security, such as fingerprint, face recognition, and it is already difficult to break into the devices with these security measurements. Integrating AI to information security could help prevent leaking sensitive information, for both internet users and organizations. Take Gmail for example, Google already using AI to block spam emails every day.

The list of what AI could do is getting longer every year, and there are some features could be integrated into many security systems, reducing human-error.

- Self–learning systems. AI in cyber security allows a system to learn as it grows, self-correcting become more effective and efficient.

- Predicting breach risk. With self-learning, the system can learn to predict the risk of breach in a wider scale and prioritize those risks, allowing human to focus on the most serious ones.

- Detecting gaps. It is also extremely challenging to test for potential security gaps in large and complex networks manually; on the other hand, AI can handle it. It is now possible for a security system to be frequently updated and get the latest security patches.

Automatic threat detection. With AI, threats can be detected before they become costly. The security system can potentially be trained to see a DDoS attack beforehand.

Before AI is normalized in security, there are a few steps my family and I can take to protect ourselves from the attackers, such as updating software and operating system, using anti-virus software, using strong passwords and make sure my family members don't use their names and birthday to create a password, avoid using public WiFi networks that are not protected by passwords.
